# Example Prompts

Ready-to-use prompts for LinkedIn sourcing. Copy any of these and paste into Claude Code after setup.

---

## Simple search

```
Find me Senior React developers in Kyiv with 3+ years of experience
```

---

## Detailed search with filters

```
I'm looking for a Product Manager with fintech experience, based in Berlin or open to remote.
Requirements: 5+ years in product, experience with payment systems or banking.
Show me their current role, company, and a brief summary of experience.
```

---

## Search + connect

```
Search for DevOps engineers in Poland who have experience with Kubernetes and AWS.
For the top 5 matches, send a connection request with a short personalized note
that mentions their current role and why I'd like to connect.
```

---

## Sourcing for a specific vacancy

```
I have an open position: Senior Backend Developer (Python/Django), Kyiv or remote, $4000-6000.
Search LinkedIn for candidates who match. For each person show:
- Name and current position
- Company
- Years of relevant experience
- Key technologies from their profile

After reviewing results, I'll tell you who to connect with.
```

---

## Check inbox

```
Open my LinkedIn messages and show me who wrote in the last few days.
Summarize each conversation briefly.
```

---

## Review connection requests

```
Show me pending connection requests on LinkedIn.
For each person, tell me who they are and whether it's worth accepting.
```

---

## Bulk profile review

```
I have a list of LinkedIn profiles to review:
- https://www.linkedin.com/in/profile1
- https://www.linkedin.com/in/profile2
- https://www.linkedin.com/in/profile3

For each one, give me a summary: current role, experience, location,
key skills, and whether they could be a good fit for a Senior Frontend role.
```

---

## Tips

- Write in any language -- Ukrainian, English, Russian, whatever is comfortable
- Be specific about what you're looking for: the more details, the better results
- You can change search criteria mid-session: "actually, also check for Node.js developers"
- If the AI says the daily limit is reached, just continue tomorrow -- this protects your LinkedIn account
- You can ask the AI to save results: "save all found candidates to a CSV file"
